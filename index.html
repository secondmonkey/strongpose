Strongpose

Pose for 2 minutes. Perform better.

The Science

Recent science shows that posing in "high-power" postures significantly lowers cortisol levels and increases testosterone levels, as compared to "low-power" postures, and that holding these postures before a stressful event like a job interview leads to markedly better performance:

  • Carney, Dana R., Amy J.C. Cuddy, and Andy J. Yap. "Power Posing: Brief Nonverbal Displays Affect Neuroendocrine Levels and Risk Tolerance." Psychological Science 21, no. 10 (October 2010): 1363â€“1368.
  • Cuddy, Amy J.C., Caroline A. Wilmuth, and Dana R. Carney. "Preparatory Power Posing Affects Performance and Outcomes in Social Evaluations." Harvard Business School Working Paper, No. 13-027, September 2012. (Revised November 2012.)

The App

Inspired by this science, the Strongpose app guides you through a series of three postures for a total of 2 minutes (40 seconds each).

Complete the postures at your desk using the web version.

Or, for use out and about, download the iOS app here.
